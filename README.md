# insurance-analysis
This is an analysis of data gathered by an insurance. The goal is to identify the major causes of high treatment costs by patients. 

## Background Story

A health insurance company has observed significant variations in the medical charges claimed by policyholders across different demographic groups and regions. While some customers incur relatively low healthcare costs, others generate substantially higher claims, leading to increased financial pressure on the company.

To improve pricing strategies, risk assessment, and customer segmentation, management wants to better understand the factors that drive insurance charges. Specifically, they are interested in determining whether age, lifestyle choices (such as smoking), family size, body mass index (BMI), gender, and geographic location influence healthcare costs.

The company has provided historical customer data and tasked the analytics team with developing an interactive dashboard that will uncover patterns, identify high-risk customer groups, and support data-driven decision-making for future policy pricing and risk management.

---

## Problem Statement

The insurance company currently lacks a centralized system for monitoring and understanding the key factors influencing customer medical charges. As a result, management faces challenges in:

* Identifying the characteristics of customers with the highest insurance costs.
* Understanding the impact of smoking habits on healthcare expenses.
* Evaluating how age and BMI contribute to increased medical claims.
* Comparing healthcare costs across different geographical regions.
* Assessing whether family size influences insurance charges.
* Identifying what region pose higher financial risks.

Therefore, the objective of this analysis is to analyze customer demographics and lifestyle factors to uncover the major drivers of insurance charges and provide actionable insights that support strategic decision-making.

---

## Analysis Objective

The analysis should help the insurance company answer questions such as:

1. What is the average insurance charge?
2. Which age groups incur the highest medical costs?
3. How do smokers and non-smokers compare in terms of insurance charges?
4. Is there a relationship between BMI and healthcare costs?
5. Which regions have the highest average charges?
6. Does the number of children affect insurance expenses?
7. What region has the highest average insurance?
